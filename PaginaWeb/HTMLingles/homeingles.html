Welcome to Save The Plants

Save The Plants is a student group dedicated to the investigation of the environmental heritage of Granada. In this web page you can find information about some coasts of Granada, as well as images and more utilities. We hope you enjoy your time here.

Other topics of interest

The peaks of Sierra Nevada, the spectacular valley of the Castril river, the "young" coast of Granada or the great Gruyère cheese that resembles Sierra Gorda, due to its numerous chasms and cavities, make up the special list of the complex relief of the province.

Located on two adjacent hills, the Albaicín and the Alhambra form the medieval core of Granada that dominates the modern city. In the eastern part of the fortress and royal residence of the Alhambra are the wonderful gardens of the Generalife, the country house of the emirates that dominated this part of Spain in the thirteenth and fourteenth centuries. The Albaicín neighborhood preserves a rich set of Arab buildings harmoniously fused with traditional Andalusian architecture.
